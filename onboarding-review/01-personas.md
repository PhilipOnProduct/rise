# Personas for Rise onboarding review

These 5 personas are chosen to stress different parts of the current onboarding flow:
trip type chips, dynamic travel-style adaptation, budget tiers, the activity rating step,
and trust in an AI-generated itinerary. Edit freely before persona walkthroughs.

---

## 1. Mira — The Spontaneous Solo Traveler

> Renamed from "Maya" to avoid clash with the Designer in the virtual product team (TEAM.md).

- **Age / context:** 28, freelance designer, Brooklyn NY
- **Tech comfort:** Very high; mobile-first; quick to abandon clunky flows
- **Planning style:** Books trips 2–3 weeks out; hates over-planning
- **Budget:** Comfortable, but values experiences over luxury
- **Trust in AI:** High — uses LLMs daily; will give it a chance, but expects taste
- **Trip:** 4 nights solo in Lisbon next month
- **Job-to-be-done:** "Help me find places I wouldn't have found on my own, without making me sit through a 20-minute setup."
- **What she'll stress in onboarding:** Solo trip type (does it exist?), speed of flow, copy that feels generic vs. taste-aware.

---

## 2. Anjali Patel — The Family Trip Planner

- **Age / context:** 38, marketing manager, suburban Chicago. Husband + two kids (ages 7 and 11)
- **Tech comfort:** Medium-high
- **Planning style:** Heavy researcher — TripAdvisor, Reddit, mom blogs. Cross-checks everything
- **Budget:** Comfortable, with hard ceilings
- **Trust in AI:** Cautiously curious — willing to try, but will not trust kid-safety to a model
- **Trip:** 7-night family trip to Portugal during summer break
- **Job-to-be-done:** "Plan a trip the whole family will enjoy without me spending nights on TripAdvisor."
- **What she'll stress in onboarding:** Family trip type (does it exist?), kid-friendliness signals, ability to communicate constraints (allergies, ages, walking distance), trust building.

---

## 3. Marco Rinaldi — The Bleisure Business Traveler

- **Age / context:** 44, regional sales director, Milan. Travels 2x/month for work
- **Tech comfort:** High; impatient
- **Planning style:** Decides in 5 minutes. Will pay for quality. Hates forms.
- **Budget:** Flexible (premium)
- **Trust in AI:** High, but expects efficiency, not novelty
- **Trip:** 36 hours free after a conference in Lisbon next week
- **Job-to-be-done:** "I have one and a half free days — show me three excellent things to do, fast."
- **What he'll stress in onboarding:** Tolerance for steps (will he abandon at step 3?), short-trip handling, hotel-already-booked path, premium signaling.

---

## 4. Sam & Chris — The Bucket-List Couple

- **Age / context:** Mid-50s, both retired teachers, Boston
- **Tech comfort:** Medium-low; desktop users; cautious clickers
- **Planning style:** Methodical — months of research. Anxious about "getting it right"
- **Budget:** Comfortable, but disciplined
- **Trust in AI:** Skeptical; want depth, sources, authenticity
- **Trip:** 14-night Portugal + Spain trip — first big international trip in a decade
- **Job-to-be-done:** "We've waited 30 years for this trip — make sure we don't miss the important things and that it feels real, not generic."
- **What they'll stress in onboarding:** Long trip handling, multi-city support, "is this AI-slop or curated?" trust signals, accessibility/legibility (font size, contrast).

---

## 5. Tomás — The Budget Friend-Group Organizer

- **Age / context:** 24, master's student, Madrid. Organizing a trip for 4 friends
- **Tech comfort:** High; mobile-first
- **Planning style:** Group-driven, decisions need buy-in via WhatsApp
- **Budget:** Savvy
- **Trust in AI:** Medium — likes recommendations but doesn't want to feel railroaded
- **Trip:** Long weekend (3 nights) in Lisbon for someone's birthday
- **Job-to-be-done:** "Plan something the four of us can actually afford and want to do — nightlife, cheap eats, beach, no boring museum tours."
- **What he'll stress in onboarding:** Friend group trip type, budget signaling for "Savvy", relevance of activity recommendations, shareability ("can I send this to the group?").

## Why this set

| Lens | Coverage |
|---|---|
| **Trip type** | Solo (Mira), Family with kids (Anjali), Solo business (Marco), Couple (Sam & Chris), Friend group (Tomás) |
| **Tech comfort** | High, Medium-high, High, Medium-low, High |
| **Budget tier** | Comfortable, Comfortable, Flexible, Comfortable, Savvy |
| **Trust in AI** | High, Cautious, High, Skeptical, Medium |
| **Trip length** | Short, Medium, Very short, Long, Short |
| **Planning style** | Spontaneous, Methodical, Decisive, Methodical, Group-driven |

This forces the onboarding to handle the full matrix — not just the easy "couple, 7 nights, somewhere in Europe" happy path.
